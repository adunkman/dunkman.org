Boy Scout Troop 512’s Spaghetti Dinner

Yes, I was once a Boy Scout—a member of Troop 512 based out of Woodridge, Illinois. I was contacted to whip up a set of tickets and churned these out in a few hours. Surprisingly, I’m still happy with the way they turned out.

The chef graphic has been the logo of the Spaghetti Dinner for the last five years or so, and it was the only design requirement.

Graphic design community: sorry, but I just had to use Sign Painter.

Details: PDF, Printed on card stock, color, 6 per 8.5x11" portrait. Tools used: Adobe InDesign.

December, 2010 • Boy Scout Troop 512, Woodridge, Illinois

Missouri S&T Safe Space Program

A “Safe Space” is a place in which anti-LGBT violence and harassment is not tolerated and is an environment that is open and accepting of everyone. The Safe Space Program was started at Missouri S&T to train students, staff, and faculty in LGBT issues and educate them in what is offensive and what is not.

The manual was given to me in draft to revise for content, but I couldn’t help but put a fresh coat of paint on it (or, more realistically, destroy the manual’s formatting and rebuild it).

Details: PDF, Printed double-sided on plain white paper, color, 8.5x11" portrait. Tools used: Adobe InDesign, Illustrator.

December, 2009 • Missouri S&T, Leadership and Cultural Programs, Rolla, Missouri

Missouri S&T DaVinci Society

The DaVinci Society is a student organization for gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, queer, and questioning students, staff, and faculty, along with supportive allies (mouthful, amirite?).

When I entered the organization, their website was years out-of-date, and I wasn’t sure they still existed. After a year of attending meetings, I took over their website in order to bring a fresher look and more up-to-date content. I started by analyzing what the website was currently being used for, and drew up a new design that was a lot more colorful (it is the “gay club,” after all) and organized.

Although I no longer maintain the website, the organization continues to use and update the design with little or no modification.

Available online at http://web.mst.edu/~davinci.

Details: Website. Tools used: Adobe Photoshop, CSS and HTML, Apache SSIs.

August, 2009 • Missouri S&T, DaVinci Society, Rolla, Missouri
